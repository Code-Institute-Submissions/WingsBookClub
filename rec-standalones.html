About Stand-Alones

While I love reading series, there are occasionally times when I don't feel up to reading a full series. These stand-alone books are great for those times when you just want an easy read without having to remember too much of the plot for the next part.

As I've mentioned in the introduction, I especially love these for lazy sunny weekends in the garden. They're great company to take on holiday as well as you can pack in a few different stories and thus have more of your fictional friends by your side at the pool!

Recommendations

Stand-Alones

Woman To Woman

by Cathy Kelly

I think I might be addicted to this book. I've bought it at a second-hand shop over ten years ago and have read it at least once a year since then!

It probably helps that one of the two main characters is my namesake and hails from County Sligo in Ireland, my second home. Reading it the first time felt like going home, so it's always on standby for when I get 'homesick' for Ireland.

The Little Shop of Happy Ever After

by Jenny Colgan

A more recent favourite, this story is about a booklover and librarian who gets made redundant. She hates to leave the books she loves so much behind and finds a way to keep matching books to the right readers.

A truly inspirational tale about making the best of a bad situation.
